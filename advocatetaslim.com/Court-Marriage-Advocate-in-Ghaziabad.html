Understanding Court Marriage in India

A court marriage is performed under the Special Marriage Act, 1954, which allows two individuals, regardless of their religion, caste, or creed, to marry legally. Unlike traditional weddings, court marriage doesn’t require religious rituals — it’s a civil contract between two consenting adults. The process is recognized by law and provides the couple with a valid marriage certificate that serves as legal proof of their union. If you’re residing in Ghaziabad and looking for a trusted legal advisor to guide you through the procedure, Advocate Taslim offers expert legal assistance for a seamless and quick court marriage registration.

Why Choose Court Marriage?

Court marriage is ideal for couples who want a simple, cost-effective, and legally recognized union. Here are some key benefits:

  • No Religious Boundaries: It allows inter-caste and inter-religion marriages without social barriers.
  • Transparent Procedure: The process follows clear legal steps and documentation.

Procedure for Court Marriage in Ghaziabad

Here’s a step-by-step overview of how Advocate Taslim assists couples with their court marriage registration in Ghaziabad:

  1. Notice of Intended Marriage:

    2. The first step is to file a notice at the Marriage Registrar’s Office where at least one partner has resided for 30 days. Advocate Taslim helps in drafting and submitting this notice properly.

  2. Publication and Objection Period:

    3. Once the notice is filed, it’s displayed publicly for 30 days to invite any objections. If no objections are raised, the process moves forward smoothly.

  3. Documents Verification:

    4. Advocate Taslim ensures that all documents — such as age proof, address proof, passport-sized photos, and affidavits — are verified correctly to avoid any delays.

  4. Declaration and Witnesses:

    5. Both parties, along with three witnesses, must sign a declaration before the Marriage Officer. Advocate Taslim assists in arranging witnesses and preparing all required affidavits.

  5. Marriage Registration:

    6. After successful verification, the marriage is solemnized in the presence of the Marriage Officer and witnesses. The couple receives a legal marriage certificate, completing the process.

Documents Required for Court Marriage

Here’s a quick checklist of documents you’ll need, as guided by Advocate Taslim:

  • Proof of age (Birth Certificate, 10th Certificate, or Passport)
  • Proof of residence (Aadhar Card, Voter ID, or Utility Bill)
  • Passport-size photographs of both partners
  • Affidavit confirming marital status, date of birth, and nationality
  • Divorce decree (if previously married) or death certificate (if widow/widower)
  • Three witnesses with their valid ID proofs
